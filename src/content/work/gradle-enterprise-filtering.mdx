[Gradle](https://gradle.com) builds developer productivity tools. Engineering teams at Netflix and Salesforce use Gradle Enterprise (now Develocity) to keep their CI/CD pipelines healthy. As lead designer, I owned how those teams diagnosed slow tests and tracked down flaky failures across thousands of daily builds.

The filtering pattern replaces a dozen scattered dropdowns with one typed input. You describe what you're looking for and the omnibar offers filter conditions you can stack. The signal we couldn't ignore: customers were exporting to CSV and slicing data in spreadsheets, because in-product filtering wasn't covering their queries.

The first concept was a clickable visual query builder. Too slow for experts, too opaque for beginners. We switched to a typed input that worked for both.

## Typed suggestions surface the taxonomy

A new user types "slow" and the omnibar offers "duration greater than" and "status: failed" as next steps. Filter types become discoverable through use rather than through documentation.

## Implicit AND across stacked filters

Most diagnostic queries are AND chains: "slow tests AND failed AND in module X." V1 defaulted to implicit AND and skipped the boolean builder. That covered the patterns engineers actually used without the cost of a full query language.

We prototyped against Gradle's real build event data instead of fixtures, so the edge cases — large datasets, odd chains where suggestions misfired — surfaced early. Engineering caught the failure modes in the prototype and fixed them before V1.

## A pattern that fed every diagnostic view

The same filter chain drove the scoped task list, the diagnostic detail, and the summary roll-ups. Once you learned the omnibar on the build list, the same interaction worked across the rest of the product.

## Results

Customers stopped asking for CSV exports as their queries moved back into the product. After a customer asked to use the omnibar on more selectors, other feature teams picked it up. It became the filter language for Gradle Enterprise instead of a one-view feature.
