Join is like Jira for construction. Contractors and owners use it to collaborate on the decisions that move a project forward.

Construction projects live and die on bundles of decisions, not individual choices. A contractor doesn't decide "which countertop" in isolation. They decide "which finish package for kitchen and bathrooms" against budget and schedule. Before Scenarios, Join asked users to think about decisions one at a time. That didn't match how real decisions got made.

## The product

<FullBleedImage
  src={joinScenarios01}
  alt="Join Scenarios overview listing two alternative versions of a construction project"
/>

**Sets of decisions as the unit.** A contractor models a "premium finishes, compressed schedule" option alongside a "standard finishes, stretched schedule" option, and the math propagates across every connected line item automatically. The owner sees two packages, not forty decisions.

<FullBleedImage
  src={joinScenarios02}
  alt="Scenarios comparison view showing two packaged project options side-by-side with their cost and schedule trade-offs"
/>

**Tabular comparison without ranking.** This was the hardest design call. Sophisticated owners on multi-million-dollar projects don't want to be steered toward a "best" option. They want to weigh the dimensions that matter to them. The comparison is deliberately flat. It surfaces every trade-off without marking any option as preferred.

**Progressive disclosure.** The default comparison view is lean on purpose. Users click into specific dimensions for detail. The owner-facing read stays calm during a sign-off meeting. The contractor-facing detail stays rich for internal work.

## Results

The scenario-decision cycle dropped 50%, measured from creation through owner sign-off. The surprise was the architect audience. 20% of Scenarios users were architects, a segment Join hadn't been designing for. The comparison view matched how they presented design options to their own clients, and they found the feature on their own. That discovery reshaped how Join thought about the product's reach.
