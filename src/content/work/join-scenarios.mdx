Side-by-side comparison for construction project decisions. A contractor doesn't pick countertops in isolation. They weigh "premium finishes, compressed schedule" against "standard finishes, stretched schedule" as a package. Join was forcing one-at-a-time decisions. That's not how the work happens.

(Previous approach: individual line-item toggles. Owners couldn't see the compounding effect of forty separate choices. Reframed as linked sets.)

The reframe: decisions as linked sets, not individual line items.

## Sets of decisions as the unit

Model two packages. Math propagates across every connected line item. The owner sees two options, not forty trade-offs.

## Tabular comparison without ranking

Hardest design call. Owners on multi-million-dollar projects don't want to be steered. The comparison is deliberately flat. Every trade-off surfaced. No option marked preferred.

## Progressive disclosure

Default view is lean. Click into specific dimensions for detail. Owner-facing read stays calm for sign-off meetings. Contractor-facing detail stays rich for internal work.

## What moved

Decision cycle dropped 50%, measured from creation through owner sign-off. The surprise: 20% of users were architects. Join hadn't designed for them. The comparison view matched how architects present design options to their own clients. They found it on their own.
