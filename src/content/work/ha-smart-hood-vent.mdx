Automated range hood that detects cooking in 30-60 seconds. Gas stoves emit real pollutants. The fix is simple: turn on your hood. The problem: most people forget.

Most smart hood automations use a temperature threshold. Two problems. Slow: by the time the kitchen heats up, you've been breathing pollutants for 5-10 minutes. Unreliable: hot summer days false-trigger or need seasonal recalibration.

<Callout label="The core insight">
  A threshold system asks "is it hot?" Rate-of-change detection asks "is it getting hotter?" The second question catches cooking within 30-60 seconds. Same data, different question.
</Callout>

Burner on, temperature derivative spikes within a rolling two-minute window. Cooking stops, rate flattens, hood shuts off. Hot days don't false-trigger because temperature is high but stable.

## How it works

Watches both temperature and humidity rate of change. Temperature catches most cooking. Humidity catches boiling water, where steam arrives before heat.

**UI-adjustable thresholds.** Every sensitivity setting exposed as a dashboard slider. Tune without editing YAML.

**Manual override.** Toggle the hood by hand, automation steps back for 30 minutes.

**Safety shutoff.** Two hours continuous: forces off, posts notification.
