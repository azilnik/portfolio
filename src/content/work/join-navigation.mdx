## The Challenge

Join's hiring platform had grown organically over several years, and the navigation
reflected that history. Key features were buried in nested menus, users couldn't
find what they needed quickly, and onboarding new team members took too long.

Our analytics showed that users averaged **4.2 clicks** to reach their most-used
features, and support tickets about "where do I find X?" were one of the top
three categories.

<Callout label="Key Insight">
  Users had developed workarounds — bookmarking deep links and sharing direct URLs
  in Slack. The navigation wasn't just inconvenient, it was invisible to many users.
</Callout>

## Research & Discovery

I led a research sprint that included:

- **15 user interviews** with hiring managers, recruiters, and executives
- **Heatmap analysis** of existing navigation usage patterns
- **Card sorting sessions** to understand users' mental models
- **Competitive audit** of 8 similar platforms

The research revealed three core problems: information hierarchy didn't match user
priorities, terminology was inconsistent, and the mobile experience was essentially
broken.

## Design Solution

We restructured the entire navigation around user tasks rather than system features.
The key changes were:

1. **Flat hierarchy** — reduced from 3 levels to 2
2. **Role-based defaults** — the nav adapts based on whether you're a recruiter,
   hiring manager, or executive
3. **Quick actions bar** — one-click access to the 5 most common tasks
4. **Global search** — a command-palette-style search that finds anything

## Results

The redesign shipped in Q3 2024 after 6 weeks of development. Key results
measured after 30 days:

- **Task completion rate** improved by 28%
- **Average time-to-task** dropped from 4.2 clicks to 1.8 clicks
- **Support tickets** about navigation decreased by 62%
- **User satisfaction** (measured via in-app survey) rose from 3.1 to 4.6/5
