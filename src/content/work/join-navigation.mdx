[Join](https://join.build) is project management for the people who build actual buildings. As Head of Design, I lead a small team and ship product across the platform.

After four years of growing a feature at a time, our nav had labels in it that no customer ever used out loud. CS would forward me yet another "how do I get to X" ticket on the regular. I spent a few weeks in usability sessions asking customers to describe Join back to us in their own words. Not one of them used our labels. Everyone talked about a project, what phase it was in, who they were chasing for an answer. So we threw out the IA and rebuilt the whole nav around the words they were using. The portfolio sits up top. A project is the unit underneath. Any deep view is two clicks away from the portfolio in either direction.

## Projects as the top-level unit

The portfolio is the entry point. Click into it and you're inside a project. Features hang off the project and reorder themselves based on the phase of work — precon stuff comes up first while the project is in precon, RFIs and change orders come up first once the project moves into build. We pull the phase signal off the project itself, so a PM doesn't have to find a setting to flip when the work shifts under them.

## A two-click zoom

From the portfolio, you're two clicks away from any view inside a project, and the same two clicks back. Zooming in had always worked fine. The trip back up the hierarchy was where people got stuck — it used to mean hunting through a nav that had grown by accretion. Once both directions took the same number of clicks, the complaint mostly stopped showing up.

## Features in their work context

When a PM opens a project that's already in build, the nav shows them change orders and RFIs at the top, and the estimating tools they last touched six months ago aren't anywhere they have to scroll past. The right feature surfaces wherever the work is happening, not in the order we happened to ship things over the past four years.

## Results

Navigation tickets dropped 32% after we launched. As an unexpected bonus, customers started using the org-level portfolio views for the first time, even though those views had been in the product the whole time. The way back into a specific project from up there had been a guessing game in the old nav, so most people had just stopped going up to the org level.
