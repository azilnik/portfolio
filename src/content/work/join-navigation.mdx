Navigation rebuild around projects. The old surface had grown feature-by-feature. Labels drifted from how users described their work. Support was absorbing "how do I get to X" tickets. Usability sessions confirmed it: users described the app in terms of projects, not features.

New rhythm: zoom in to one project, zoom out across the portfolio. Two clicks either direction.

## Projects as the top-level unit

Not a list of features. The portfolio, then the project. Features nest as sub-navigation inside the project's context, sequenced by phase.

## Two-click zoom rhythm

Portfolio overview to any project view: two clicks. Deep inside a project back out: two clicks. Symmetric.

  src={joinNav03}

## Feature discovery as a side effect

Landing on a project surfaces features relevant to that phase. Discovery happens naturally. No onboarding needed.

## What moved

Support tickets on navigation dropped 32%. Users started reaching portfolio views they'd never touched. The zoom-out existed in the old nav. Nobody used it because there was no clear path back in.
