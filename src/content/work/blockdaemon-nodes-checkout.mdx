Blockdaemon is like AWS for crypto. I joined as the founding designer after [Blockdaemon acquired my consultancy, Gravy](https://www.blockdaemon.com/blog/announcing-blockdaemons-acquisition-of-gravy).

Nodes Checkout is a self-serve purchase flow. The goal: deploy a blockchain node as easily as you'd spin up an AWS server. Before this, customers filed a support ticket and waited days or weeks while sales handled it. The new flow works in minutes.

## Purchase metaphor at every step

Every screen reads like Shopify, not a server config wizard. Node deployment maps to a procurement flow because that's how our users were already buying infrastructure.

## Chain-aware progressive disclosure

Bitcoin, Ethereum, and Solana don't share a config schema. The checkout hides everything except what your chain actually needs and pre-fills smart defaults. You only see what matters for the chain you picked.

<FullBleedImage
  src={bdCheckout02}
  alt="Chain selection and network tier view inside the Blockdaemon checkout"
/>

## Confirming user intent

Before checkout, you see the cost, configuration, and the SLA Blockdaemon stands behind. That detail gave teams confidence they were buying the right thing — and meant fewer support tickets once nodes were live.

## Results

Usage grew 23x in the first year. DevOps teams went from weekly support tickets to deploying nodes in minutes.
