Self-serve purchase flow for blockchain node deployment. Before: support ticket, days-to-weeks sales cycle, engineering time burned per customer. After: minutes.

(First concept was a configuration wizard. Customers associated wizards with forms, not decisions. Reframed as a purchase. Self-serve pattern landed without instruction.)

## Purchase metaphor at every step

Every screen reads like e-commerce. DevOps engineers already know procurement flows. Mapping node deployment to that model removed the instruction barrier.

## Chain-aware progressive disclosure

Every blockchain has its own configuration shape. The checkout surfaces only the fields relevant to the selected chain. Sensible defaults. No full-matrix learning curve.

<FullBleedImage
  src={bdCheckout02}
  alt="Chain selection and network tier view inside the Blockdaemon checkout"
/>

## Review step that earned the commit

Cost, configuration, SLA. Procurement signs off on a review screen. Can't sign off on a half-filled form.

<FullBleedImage
  src={bdCheckout03}

## What moved

23x usage growth in year one. Enterprise conversations shifted from "how do we get you a node" to "how many nodes and which chains." Growth decoupled from sales capacity.
