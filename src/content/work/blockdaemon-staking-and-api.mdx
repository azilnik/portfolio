Blockdaemon is like AWS for crypto. Exchanges and institutional custodians use it to deploy nodes and stake assets without operating their own data centers, and enterprises like Goldman Sachs and Microsoft are the customer tier.

I joined the Blockdaemon team as the founding designer after [Blockdaemon acquired my consultancy, Gravy](https://www.blockdaemon.com/blog/announcing-blockdaemons-acquisition-of-gravy). The company raised $207M at a $3.25B valuation during my tenure, and there was no existing design organization when I arrived.

Over 15 months we shipped two zero-to-one products: the institutional staking dashboard and the Ubiquity developer API. Neither had established UX patterns in crypto, so we stood up the research practice to inform both.

## Institutional staking

The staking dashboard was zero-to-one with no competitor design patterns to reference. The users were institutional treasury teams managing portfolios worth hundreds of millions across multiple blockchains, and they needed total value and validator performance on one screen.

We hired Blockdaemon's first two researchers and stood up the research practice from scratch. Their interviews with treasury teams shaped the design direction toward density plus real-time trust signals. These users weren't trying to avoid information; they wanted to see all of it on one surface without losing the signal.

## Ubiquity API

Ubiquity was Blockdaemon's developer-facing API for querying crypto data across chains. The dashboard was the first place developers went to debug what their API keys were doing. The highest-value views turned out to be usage against rate limits and request-log auditing — both surfaced in developer interviews.

## Results

The staking product shipped into the $207M Series C materials and was part of the investor demo flow when the round closed. We grew the design organization to seven people and set up design reviews and a weekly research cadence for the first time at the company.
