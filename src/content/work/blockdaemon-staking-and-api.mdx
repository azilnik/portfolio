Crypto infrastructure companies treat design as a downstream concern — styling the API docs, polishing the marketing site. The argument here is that infrastructure products have design problems at their core. The decisions about what to show, what to hide, and how to build trust with institutional money are design decisions. They just don't look like design decisions until someone does the research.

## The situation

Blockdaemon is like AWS for crypto. Exchanges and institutional custodians use it to deploy nodes and stake assets without operating their own data centers. Goldman Sachs and Microsoft are the customer tier.

I joined as the founding designer after Blockdaemon acquired my consultancy. The company raised $207M at a $3.25B valuation during my tenure. There was no design organization. I was the design org.

Neither the institutional staking dashboard nor the Ubiquity developer API had established UX patterns in crypto. No competitor had solved these. No one had tried to solve them with research.

## The wrong direction

The default for crypto dashboards in 2021 was retail: clean, minimal, lots of whitespace. That's what the industry knew. But our users weren't retail investors checking their portfolio on a phone. They were institutional treasury teams managing hundreds of millions across multiple blockchains. Simplification wasn't a virtue for them — it was a liability. Hiding information meant hiding risk.

## The bet

We hired Blockdaemon's first two researchers and stood up the research practice from scratch. Their interviews with treasury teams changed the design direction entirely. What those users wanted was density plus real-time trust signals. They weren't trying to avoid information. They were trying to see all of it without drowning in it.

## What happened

The staking product shipped into the $207M Series C materials. When the round closed, the screens investors walked through in those demos traced back to my team. I grew the design org from one to seven people. We set up design reviews with real teeth and a weekly research cadence for the first time.

## The remaining argument

Density-first worked for institutional treasury teams. The open question is whether that approach holds as staking products move downstream to smaller funds and individual high-net-worth clients. Those users may actually want the simplified retail patterns I rejected. The bet was context-specific, not universal — and the next version of this product will need to serve both tiers without a mode switch. I'd also invest earlier in the design system. Scaling from one to seven designers without shared components cost us speed in the second year that we'd built in the first.
