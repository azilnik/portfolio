Blockdaemon is like AWS for crypto. Exchanges and institutional custodians use it to deploy nodes and stake assets without operating their own data centers. Enterprises like Goldman Sachs and Microsoft are the customer tier. The company raised $207M at a $3.25B valuation during my tenure.

I joined as the founding designer after Blockdaemon acquired my consultancy. There was no design organization. I was the design org.

## Node deployment checkout

Deploying a blockchain node used to mean filing a support ticket and waiting on a sales cycle. The backend could already deploy nodes in minutes. The interface was the bottleneck.

<Callout label="The insight">
  Customers weren't asking for better configuration. They were asking to buy a thing instead of provisioning one.
</Callout>

We replaced the infrastructure-management flow with a purchase checkout. The left side walks through configuration in logical groups. The right side builds a live order summary with real-time pricing and a single "Subscribe" action.

Monthly usage grew 23x. Customers deployed in minutes without engineering help. Enterprise conversations shifted from "how do we get you a node" to "how many nodes and which chains."

## Institutional staking

The staking dashboard was zero-to-one. No competitor had established the design patterns. Institutional users manage portfolios worth hundreds of millions across multiple blockchains and need to see total value and validator performance on one screen.

We hired Blockdaemon's first two researchers and stood up the research practice from scratch. Their interviews with institutional treasury teams changed the design direction. What those users wanted was density plus real-time trust signals. They weren't trying to avoid information. They were trying to see all of it without drowning in it.

Node deployment usage grew 23x. The marketing site overhaul lifted click-through rates 64%. When the $207M Series C closed, the screens investors walked through in those demos all traced back to my team. The checkout became the reference pattern for subsequent product launches.
