AI engine that surfaces cost-saving material alternatives inside construction estimates. An estimator specs a finish at full cost when a cheaper substitute exists. That gap compounds across every line item. Ideas closes it automatically.

(Original plan: materials marketplace. Customer interviews killed it. Estimators didn't know what to search for. Pivoted to recommendations. Led design + acting PM. Shipped in two months.)

## Suggestion carousels

AI-generated suggestions ranked by savings potential. Grouped by estimate location and construction phase. Each carousel scopes to a single section of the estimate so reviewers process decisions in context, not as a disconnected feed.

<FullBleedImage
  src={joinIdeas02}
  alt="Join Ideas browsing page with two suggestion carousels: items found in the estimate and items commonly considered at this construction phase"
/>

## Human review layer

Suggestions affect real buildings. A materials researcher validates every recommendation before it reaches an estimator. The model drafts. Humans approve. Rejection reasons feed back into the model so the same bad suggestion doesn't recur.

Each card shows UniFormat and MasterFormat codes. Non-negotiable for trust in construction. Estimators verify specs against these codes daily; omitting them would have killed adoption.

<FullBleedImage
  src={joinIdeas04}
  alt="Three Join Ideas suggestion cards showing alternate materials with dollar amounts and construction classification codes"
/>

## Forced comparison

Every recommendation weighed against alternatives before acceptance. The model suggests. The estimator decides. Nothing auto-applies.

<FullBleedImage
  src={joinIdeas03}

## What moved

One enterprise customer: $500K saved on their first estimate within 30 days. Decision time down 50% across the product.
