Construction estimators lose money they don't know about. A less experienced estimator specs a finish at full cost when an alternate would work for half. The gap is institutional knowledge, and it compounds across every project.

Leadership wanted to solve this with a materials marketplace. Customer research showed estimators don't browse catalogs. They don't have time, and they don't know what to search for. What they need is for the right alternatives to surface inside the workflow they already trust.

## The product

Join Ideas surfaces AI-generated suggestions as contextual carousels ranked by cost-saving potential. The design problem isn't the carousels. It's what to do when the LLM is wrong. These are decisions that shape real buildings.

<FullBleedImage
  src={joinIdeas02}
  alt="Join Ideas browsing page with two suggestion carousels: items found in the estimate and items commonly considered at this construction phase"
/>

**A human curation layer.** A materials researcher validates every LLM suggestion before it reaches an estimator. The LLM is a first-draft engine, not the source of truth. This made the system trustworthy on day one.

**Provenance by classification code.** Each card shows UniFormat and MasterFormat codes. Estimators told us the codes were non-negotiable for trust. Without them, they wouldn't take a suggestion seriously.

<FullBleedImage
  src={joinIdeas04}
  alt="Three Join Ideas suggestion cards showing alternate materials with dollar amounts and construction classification codes"
/>

**Forced side-by-side comparison.** Every recommendation has to be weighed against alternatives before an estimator can accept it. The model suggests. The human decides. Nothing gets auto-applied.

## Results

Within 30 days of launch, one enterprise customer saved $500K on their first project estimate. That wasn't a prediction. It was money the customer would have spent and didn't. Decision-making time dropped 50% across the product.
