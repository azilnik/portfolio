## The problem

Join Ideas started as a materials marketplace. Research changed everything.

Construction estimators miss cost-saving opportunities not because they're careless — but because they can't know what they've never seen. A less experienced estimator might not realize a newer material can do the same job for a fraction of the cost. That gap in institutional knowledge compounds across every project they touch.

<Callout label="The research pivot">
  We came in expecting to design a materials marketplace. Interviews and direct shadowing of estimators reframed it entirely. Users didn't need a place to browse — they needed a tool that would surface what they didn't know to look for, directly inside the workflow they already trusted.
</Callout>

<FullBleedImage src={joinIdeas02} alt="Join Ideas homepage showing suggestion carousels organized by cost-saving potential" />

## How it works

Join Ideas surfaces AI-generated suggestions as contextual carousels, ranked by cost-saving potential and scoped to the specific project at hand. A materials researcher curates and validates the underlying data. One click adds a suggestion to the project as a formal line item to present to the owner or client — and if approved, it flows into the drawings and plans for the building. These decisions affect real structures where people live and work.

## The impact

We released to 25% of customers. In the first 30 days, beta users accepted $500K in cost savings across their construction projects. Our internal adoption benchmark was 30% in week one — we hit 50%.

With real money on the line, opacity wasn't an option. We built an AI transparency report so users always understand how their project data is used and whether it contributes to suggestions for others.
